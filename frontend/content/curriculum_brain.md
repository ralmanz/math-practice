## SECTION 1 — CAMBRIDGE LOWER SECONDARY MATHEMATICS
**Framework: 0862 (2020) and 1112 (2018)**
**Stages: 7, 8, 9 | Ages: ~11–14**
**Algebra strand codes: Ae (Expressions, equations, formulae), As (Sequences, functions, graphs)**

### CAMBRIDGE STAGE 7 — Algebra
**Age group:** ~11–12 | **Nuvo Math grade value:** "7"

#### What IS covered at Stage 7:
- Using letters to represent unknown numbers or variables
- Understanding the words: term, expression, equation
- Knowing algebraic operations follow the same order as arithmetic (BODMAS)
- Constructing simple algebraic expressions from words
- Simplifying linear expressions: collecting like terms
- Expanding a constant over a single bracket (e.g. 3(x + 2) = 3x + 6)
- Deriving and using simple formulae (e.g. converting hours to minutes)
- Substituting positive integers into simple linear expressions/formulae
- Constructing and solving simple linear equations with integer coefficients,
  unknown on ONE side only (e.g. 2x = 8, 3x + 5 = 14, 9 – 2x = 7)
- Generating terms of integer sequences; finding term-to-term rules
- Generating sequences from spatial patterns; describing general term in simple cases
- Representing simple functions using words, symbols and mappings
- Generating coordinate pairs for linear equations where y is given explicitly in x
- Plotting straight-line graphs parallel to x- or y-axis

#### What is NOT yet covered at Stage 7:
- Negative number substitution
- Unknowns on BOTH sides of an equation
- Expanding double brackets
- Factorising
- Rearranging formulae (changing the subject)
- nth term of arithmetic sequences (formal algebraic rule)
- Simultaneous equations
- Inequalities
- Quadratic expressions

#### Vocabulary expected at Stage 7:
term, expression, equation, formula, variable, coefficient, like terms, expand, bracket,
substitute, sequence, term-to-term rule, function, mapping, coordinate, linear

#### Common misconceptions to avoid in problems:
- Confusing expressions with equations (3x + 2 is an expression; 3x + 2 = 8 is an equation)
- Sign errors when collecting like terms with negative coefficients
- Forgetting to multiply all terms inside a bracket when expanding

### CAMBRIDGE STAGE 8 — Algebra
**Age group:** ~12–13 | **Nuvo Math grade value:** "8"

#### What IS covered at Stage 8:
- All Stage 7 content (assumed prerequisite)
- Simplifying/transforming linear expressions with integer coefficients
- Collecting like terms including negative and mixed variable terms
- Expanding single brackets and simplifying (e.g. 3(2x + 5) = 6x + 15)
- Expanding and simplifying two or more single brackets (e.g. 2(x+3) + 3(x-1))
- Factorising: extracting common factors into a single bracket (e.g. 6x + 9 = 3(2x + 3))
- Deriving and using formulae (e.g. temperature conversion °C to °F)
- Substituting positive AND negative integers into formulae and expressions
- Substituting into expressions involving small powers (e.g. 3x² + 4, 2x³)
- Constructing and solving linear equations with integer coefficients,
  unknown on EITHER or BOTH sides (e.g. 3x + 2 = x + 8)
- Understanding and describing nth term rules algebraically
  (in the form n ± a, a × n, or an ± b where a and b are positive or negative integers)
- Generating coordinate pairs and plotting graphs of linear functions
- Understanding and using y = mx + c (gradient and intercept)
- Finding the gradient of a straight line from a graph or two points

#### What is NOT yet covered at Stage 8:
- Expanding double brackets (x + 2)(x + 3) — this is Stage 9
- Factorising trinomials (x² + bx + c) — this is Stage 9
- Solving simultaneous equations — this is Stage 9
- Inequalities (formal solving) — this is Stage 9
- Quadratic equations — this is Stage 9
- Negative or fractional indices — beyond scope
- Rearranging formulae with more than two steps — mostly Stage 9

#### Difficulty calibration for Nuvo Math Levels:
- **Level 1:** Basic like terms (same variable, positive coefficients), simple one-step equations
- **Level 2:** Mixed like terms (multiple variables, negative coefficients), two-step equations
- **Level 3:** Expanding single brackets + simplifying, equations with unknowns both sides
- **Level 4:** Multi-bracket expansion, factorising common factors, substitution with powers

#### Vocabulary expected at Stage 8:
All Stage 7 vocabulary plus: simplify, transform, factorise, common factor, gradient,
intercept, y = mx + c, nth term, arithmetic sequence, substitute (negative values), power

#### Common misconceptions to avoid:
- Sign errors when expanding: 3(x – 4) = 3x – 12, NOT 3x + 12
- Forgetting that factorising is the inverse of expanding
- Treating x² terms as like terms with x terms
- Gradient confusion: rise over run, not run over rise

### CAMBRIDGE STAGE 9 — Algebra
**Age group:** ~13–14 | **Nuvo Math grade value:** "9"

#### What IS covered at Stage 9:
- All Stage 7 and 8 content (assumed prerequisite)
- Expanding double brackets: (x + a)(x + b)
- Special products: perfect squares (x + a)², difference of two squares (x + a)(x – a)
- Factorising trinomials: x² + bx + c into double brackets
- Factorising difference of two squares: x² – 9 = (x+3)(x–3)
- Solving linear equations with brackets and fractions
- Rearranging formulae (changing the subject) with two or more steps
- Rearranging formulae involving powers and roots (e.g. v² = u² + 2as → solve for s)
- Solving simultaneous linear equations: elimination and substitution methods
- Linear inequalities: solving, representing on number lines
- Generating linear and quadratic sequences; identifying quadratic patterns
- Functions: domain/range, composite functions (introductory)
- Graphs: finding equation of a line, parallel lines, simple quadratic graphs

#### What is NOT yet covered at Stage 9:
- Quadratic formula — IGCSE level
- Completing the square — IGCSE level
- Cubic functions — beyond scope
- Trigonometry — geometry strand

#### Difficulty calibration for Nuvo Math Levels:
- **Level 1:** Double bracket expansion, trinomial factorising (leading coefficient 1)
- **Level 2:** Special products, harder factorising, simultaneous by substitution
- **Level 3:** Simultaneous by elimination, rearranging multi-step formulae
- **Level 4:** Inequalities, quadratic sequences, functions, word problems requiring system setup

#### Vocabulary expected at Stage 9:
All previous vocabulary plus: trinomial, double brackets, perfect square, difference of two
squares, simultaneous equations, elimination, substitution, inequality, rearrange, subject,
domain, range, composite function

### COMMON CORE GRADE 6 — Algebra
**Age group:** ~11–12 | **Nuvo Math grade value:** "6"

#### What IS covered at Grade 6:
- Writing and evaluating numerical expressions with whole-number exponents (6.EE.1)
- Writing, reading, evaluating expressions where letters stand for numbers (6.EE.2)
  - Writing expressions from verbal descriptions (e.g. "subtract y from 5" → 5 – y)
  - Identifying parts of expressions: sum, term, product, factor, quotient, coefficient
  - Evaluating expressions at specific variable values including order of operations
- Applying properties of operations to generate equivalent expressions (6.EE.3)
  - Distributive property: 3(2 + x) = 6 + 3x; 24x + 18y = 6(4x + 3y)
- Identifying equivalent expressions (6.EE.4)
- Understanding solving equations/inequalities as finding values that make them true (6.EE.5)
- Using variables to represent unknowns in real-world problems (6.EE.6)
- Solving one-step equations: x + p = q and px = q, nonnegative rational numbers (6.EE.7)
- Writing inequalities of form x > c or x < c; representing on number lines (6.EE.8)
- Representing quantitative relationships between variables with equations (6.EE.9)

#### What is NOT yet covered at Grade 6:
- Multi-step equations (two or more operations)
- Unknowns on both sides
- Negative coefficient equations
- Formal function notation
- Solving inequalities algebraically (only understanding and basic representation)
- Systems of equations

#### Key difference from Cambridge Stage 7:
Common Core Grade 6 introduces inequalities (basic notation and number line) much earlier.
Also stronger emphasis on real-world context and distributive property as a tool.

#### Difficulty calibration for Nuvo Math Levels:
- **Level 1:** Writing expressions from words, evaluating by substitution, identifying parts
- **Level 2:** Equivalent expressions using distributive property, one-step equations
- **Level 3:** Solving one-step equations with rational numbers, basic inequalities
- **Level 4:** Multi-representation problems, real-world equation setup and solve

#### Vocabulary expected at Grade 6:
variable, expression, equation, inequality, coefficient, term, factor, product, quotient,
equivalent, distributive property, substitution, evaluate, exponent

---

### COMMON CORE GRADE 7 — Algebra
**Age group:** ~12–13 | **Nuvo Math grade value:** "7"

#### What IS covered at Grade 7:
- Applying properties of operations to add, subtract, factor, expand linear expressions
  with rational coefficients (7.EE.1) — includes combining like terms with fractions/decimals
- Rewriting expressions in equivalent forms to understand problem situations (7.EE.2)
- Solving multi-step real-world problems with positive and negative rational numbers (7.EE.3)
- Solving equations of the form px + q = r and p(x + q) = r (7.EE.4a)
  - Fluently solving two-step equations
  - Word problems leading to these forms
- Solving inequalities of the form px + q > r or px + q < r (7.EE.4b)
  - Graphing solution set on number line
  - Interpreting in real-world context

#### What is NOT yet covered at Grade 7:
- Unknowns on both sides of equations (this is Grade 8)
- Expanding double brackets
- Factorising trinomials
- Functions and function notation
- Simultaneous equations
- Negative exponents or index laws

#### Key difference from Cambridge Stage 8:
Grade 7 goes deeper on rational number arithmetic within algebra (fractions and decimals
as coefficients). Much stronger emphasis on word problems and real-world context.
Inequalities are formally solved at Grade 7 (Cambridge does this at Stage 9).

#### Difficulty calibration for Nuvo Math Levels:
- **Level 1:** Like terms with integers, simple two-step equations (integer coefficients)
- **Level 2:** Like terms with rational coefficients, two-step equations with fractions/decimals
- **Level 3:** Equations of form p(x + q) = r, simple inequalities with number line
- **Level 4:** Multi-step word problems, compound inequalities, interpreting solutions in context

#### Vocabulary expected at Grade 7:
All Grade 6 vocabulary plus: rational number, inequality, solution set, number line,
multi-step, distribute, expand, factor (as verb), context, interpret

### COMMON CORE GRADE 8 — Algebra
**Age group:** ~13–14 | **Nuvo Math grade value:** "8"

#### What IS covered at Grade 8:
- Working with radicals and integer exponents (8.EE.1–4) — scientific notation, laws of exponents
- Solving linear equations with rational number coefficients (8.EE.7)
  - Equations whose solutions require expanding expressions using distributive property
  - Equations with variables on BOTH sides
  - Equations with infinitely many solutions or no solutions
- Analyzing and solving pairs of simultaneous linear equations (8.EE.8)
  - Understanding graphical solution (point of intersection)
  - Solving algebraically: substitution and elimination
  - Real-world problems leading to systems
- Defining, evaluating, comparing functions (8.F.1–3)
  - Understanding function as rule assigning one output per input
  - Representing functions: equations, tables, graphs, descriptions
  - Comparing functions represented in different ways
- Using functions to model linear relationships (8.F.4–5)
  - Determining rate of change and initial value from table, graph, equation, description
  - Interpreting rate of change and initial value in context
  - Sketching graphs based on verbal descriptions

#### What is NOT yet covered at Grade 8:
- Expanding double brackets (not explicitly required — this moves to HS Algebra)
- Factorising trinomials (High School Algebra I)
- Quadratic equations (High School)
- Completing the square (High School)
- Formal function notation f(x) (introduced but not heavily required at Grade 8)

#### Key difference from Cambridge Stage 9:
Grade 8 goes much deeper on functions than Cambridge Stage 9.
Laws of exponents are explicitly tested at Grade 8.
Simultaneous equations appear at same time as Cambridge Stage 9.
Factorising and expanding double brackets appear LATER (High School) in Common Core.

#### Difficulty calibration for Nuvo Math Levels:
- **Level 1:** Equations with variables both sides (integer coefficients), basic functions as input/output
- **Level 2:** Equations with distributive property, function tables and graphs
- **Level 3:** Simultaneous equations by substitution and graphing, slope-intercept form
- **Level 4:** Simultaneous by elimination, systems from word problems, comparing functions

#### Vocabulary expected at Grade 8:
All previous vocabulary plus: function, input, output, domain, range, rate of change,
initial value, slope, intercept, linear, system, simultaneous, exponent, scientific notation

## SECTION 5 — VALIDATION RULES
**Use these rules when evaluating any proposed content or problem.**

### Rule 1 — Topic gate
A problem MUST only cover topics listed under "What IS covered" for the
specified curriculum and grade. If a topic appears under "What is NOT yet
covered", the problem is REJECTED regardless of how simple it seems.

Example:
REJECTED: "Factorise x² + 5x + 6" tagged as Cambridge Stage 8 Level 2
REASON: Factorising trinomials is Stage 9 content, not Stage 8

APPROVED: "Factorise 6x + 9" tagged as Cambridge Stage 8 Level 1
REASON: Common factor factorising is explicitly Stage 8 content

### Rule 2 — Difficulty gate
Problems must match the Level descriptor for the unit:
- Level 1: Basic application of the concept, single variable, positive integers
- Level 2: Extended application, mixed signs, rational numbers, two operations
- Level 3: Combined skills, real-world setup, multi-step within one concept
- Level 4: Challenging, connects multiple concepts, may require problem formulation

Example:
REJECTED: "Solve 2(3x + 4) = 5x – 1" tagged as Level 1
REASON: Requires expanding and unknowns both sides — Level 3 minimum

APPROVED: "Solve 3x + 5 = 14" tagged as Level 1
REASON: Standard two-step equation, appropriate for Level 1

### Rule 3 — Vocabulary consistency
Problems must use vocabulary consistent with the curriculum:
- Cambridge uses: "simplify", "factorise", "expand", "solve", "substitute"
- Common Core uses: "evaluate", "equivalent expressions", "solve", "write an equation"
- IB MYP uses: "simplify", "solve", "investigate", "model", "justify"

Avoid mixing vocabulary across curricula in the same problem.

### Rule 4 — Word problem alignment
Common Core and IB MYP require real-world context in a higher proportion of problems.
Cambridge is more algebraic/formal.

At minimum:
- Cambridge: 20% of practice problems should have real-world context
- Common Core: 40% of practice problems should have real-world context
- IB MYP: 50% of practice problems should have real-world context

### Rule 5 — Hint quality
Hints must escalate from conceptual to procedural:
- Hint 1: Conceptual reminder (what strategy to use)
- Hint 2: First procedural step (what to do first)
- Hint 3: Near-complete worked step (almost the answer, student finishes)

Hints must NEVER give away the answer directly.
Hints must use curriculum-appropriate vocabulary.

### Rule 6 — Answer format
Answers must be in the simplest correct form:
- Simplified: 6x + 4 NOT 2(3x + 2) (unless factorised form is specifically requested)
- Equations: x = [value], not "x equals [value]"
- Inequalities: x > 3, represented on number line where appropriate
- Sequences: numerical value or algebraic nth term rule as appropriate

### PAA · Aritmética · Nivel 1
**Tema:** Operaciones con números reales y orden de operaciones

#### Tarjeta de concepto:
Los números reales incluyen todos los números racionales e irracionales.
Las operaciones básicas siguen propiedades específicas:

- **Conmutativa:** a + b = b + a · · · a × b = b × a
- **Asociativa:** (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
- **Distributiva:** a(b + c) = ab + ac
- **Elemento neutro:** a + 0 = a · · · a × 1 = a
- **Orden de operaciones (PEMDAS en español: PAPOMUDAS):**
  Paréntesis → Potencias → Multiplicación y División (de izquierda a derecha) → Adición y Sustracción (de izquierda a derecha)

#### Ejemplo trabajado:
**¿Cuánto es 18 + 2³ ÷ 4 × 2?**

Paso 1: Resolver la potencia → 2³ = 8
Paso 2: Multiplicación y división de izquierda a derecha → 8 ÷ 4 = 2 → 2 × 2 = 4
Paso 3: Suma → 18 + 4 = **22**

#### Problemas de práctica (6):

P1
- Enunciado: ¿Cuánto es 5 + 3 × 4?
- Respuesta: 17
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Recuerda el orden de operaciones — la multiplicación va antes que la suma.
- Pista 2: Primero calcula 3 × 4, luego suma 5.
- Pista 3: 3 × 4 = 12. Ahora suma: 5 + 12 = ?

P2
- Enunciado: ¿Cuánto es (5 + 3) × 4?
- Respuesta: 32
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Los paréntesis cambian el orden — resuelve primero lo que está adentro.
- Pista 2: Primero calcula 5 + 3, luego multiplica por 4.
- Pista 3: 5 + 3 = 8. Ahora multiplica: 8 × 4 = ?

P3
- Enunciado: ¿Cuánto es 24 ÷ 6 + 2 × 3?
- Respuesta: 10
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Hay dos operaciones del mismo nivel (÷ y ×) — resuélvelas de izquierda a derecha antes de sumar.
- Pista 2: Primero calcula 24 ÷ 6 y 2 × 3 por separado, luego suma los resultados.
- Pista 3: 24 ÷ 6 = 4 y 2 × 3 = 6. Ahora suma: 4 + 6 = ?

P4
- Enunciado: ¿Cuánto es 3² + 4 × 2 − 1?
- Respuesta: 16
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Empieza por la potencia, luego la multiplicación, luego suma y resta.
- Pista 2: 3² = 9 y 4 × 2 = 8. Ahora combínalos.
- Pista 3: 9 + 8 − 1 = ?

P5
- Enunciado: ¿Cuánto es 50 − 2³ × 3 + 1?
- Respuesta: 27
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Hay una potencia en la expresión — resuélvela primero.
- Pista 2: 2³ = 8. Ahora multiplica: 8 × 3 = 24. Luego opera con 50 y 1.
- Pista 3: 50 − 24 + 1 = ?

P6

- Respuesta: 10

- Pista 2: 4(5) = 20 y 2(5) = 10. Ahora resta.
- Pista 3: 20 − 10 = ?

### PAA · Aritmética · Nivel 2
**Tema:** Razón, proporción y porcentaje

#### Tarjeta de concepto:
- **Razón:** comparación entre dos cantidades → a:b o a/b
- **Proporción:** dos razones iguales → a/b = c/d, por lo tanto ad = bc (productos cruzados)
- **Porcentaje:** parte de 100 → x% de n = (x ÷ 100) × n
- **Descuento:** precio final = precio original × (1 − descuento expresado como decimal)
  Ejemplo: 20% de descuento → precio final = original × 0.80
- **Aumento:** precio final = precio original × (1 + aumento expresado como decimal)
  Ejemplo: 15% de aumento → precio final = original × 1.15
- **Cuidado:** dos descuentos consecutivos NO se suman — se aplican uno tras otro

#### Ejemplo trabajado:
**Un artículo cuesta $48.00 y tiene 20% de descuento. Los clientes con tarjeta reciben 10% adicional. ¿Cuánto más paga quien NO usa la tarjeta?**

Paso 1: Precio con 20% de descuento → 48 × 0.80 = $38.40
Paso 2: Precio con descuento adicional de 10% → 38.40 × 0.90 = $34.56
Paso 3: Diferencia → 38.40 − 34.56 = **$3.84**

Nota: El descuento total NO es 30% — es dos descuentos aplicados en secuencia.

#### Problemas de práctica (6):

P1
- Enunciado: ¿Cuánto es el 25% de 200?
- Respuesta: 50
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Para calcular un porcentaje, convierte el porcentaje a decimal y multiplica.
- Pista 2: 25% = 0.25. Ahora multiplica por 200.
- Pista 3: 0.25 × 200 = ?

P2
- Enunciado: Un producto cuesta $80.00 con 15% de descuento. ¿Cuál es el precio final?
- Respuesta: 68
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Un descuento del 15% significa que pagas el 85% del precio original.
- Pista 2: Multiplica el precio original por 0.85.
- Pista 3: 80 × 0.85 = ?

P3
- Enunciado: Si 3 lápices cuestan $1.50, ¿cuánto cuestan 8 lápices?
- Respuesta: 4
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Establece una proporción: 3 lápices es a $1.50 como 8 lápices es a $x.
- Pista 2: Usa productos cruzados: 3x = 1.50 × 8.
- Pista 3: 3x = 12. Divide: x = ?

P4
- Enunciado: En una clase hay 12 niñas y 18 niños. ¿Cuál es la razón de niñas al total de estudiantes?
- Respuesta: A
- Tipo: mcq
- Opciones:
  - A) 2:5
  - B) 2:3
  - C) 3:5
  - D) 1:3
- Pista 1: El total de estudiantes es 12 + 18 = 30. La razón es niñas:total.
- Pista 2: La razón es 12:30. Simplifica dividiendo ambos por el MCD.
- Pista 3: El MCD de 12 y 30 es 6. Entonces 12÷6 : 30÷6 = ?

P5
- Enunciado: Un conductor viajó 115 km el primer día y 85 km el segundo. Si recorrió el 80% de la distancia total, ¿cuántos km le faltan por recorrer?
- Respuesta: 50
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Primero calcula la distancia recorrida hasta ahora.
- Pista 2: 115 + 85 = 200 km. Eso representa el 80%. ¿Cuál es el 100%?
- Pista 3: Si 200 es el 80%, el total es 200 ÷ 0.80 = 250. Los km que faltan son 250 − 200 = ?

P6
- Enunciado: ¿Qué porcentaje de 150 es 45?
- Respuesta: 30
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Para encontrar qué porcentaje es una parte del total, divide la parte entre el total y multiplica por 100.
- Pista 2: (45 ÷ 150) × 100 = ?
- Pista 3: 45 ÷ 150 = 0.30. Ahora multiplica por 100.

### PAA · Aritmética · Nivel 3
**Tema:** Patrones numéricos y teoría de números

#### Tarjeta de concepto:
- **Divisibilidad:** un número es divisible por otro si el residuo de la división es 0
  - Divisible por 2: termina en número par
  - Divisible por 3: la suma de sus dígitos es divisible por 3
  - Divisible por 5: termina en 0 o 5
- **Factorización prima:** expresar un número como producto de números primos
  Ejemplo: 60 = 2² × 3 × 5
- **MCD (Máximo Común Divisor):** el mayor número que divide exactamente a dos o más números
  Método: factoriza ambos → toma los factores comunes con el menor exponente
- **MCM (Mínimo Común Múltiplo):** el menor número que es múltiplo de dos o más números
  Método: factoriza ambos → toma todos los factores con el mayor exponente
- **Sucesiones aritméticas:** diferencia constante entre términos → 3, 7, 11, 15... (+4)
- **Sucesiones geométricas:** razón constante entre términos → 2, 6, 18, 54... (×3)

#### Ejemplo trabajado:
**Halla el MCD y el MCM de 12 y 18.**

Paso 1: Factoriza cada número
- 12 = 2² × 3
- 18 = 2 × 3²

Paso 2: MCD → factores comunes con el menor exponente
- Factor 2: menor exponente es 2¹
- Factor 3: menor exponente es 3¹
- MCD = 2 × 3 = **6**

Paso 3: MCM → todos los factores con el mayor exponente
- Factor 2: mayor exponente es 2²
- Factor 3: mayor exponente es 3²
- MCM = 4 × 9 = **36**

#### Problemas de práctica (6):

P1
- Enunciado: ¿Cuál es la factorización prima de 60?
- Respuesta: B
- Tipo: mcq
- Opciones:
  - A) 2 × 5 × 6
  - B) 2² × 3 × 5
  - C) 2 × 30
  - D) 4 × 15
- Pista 1: La factorización prima usa SOLO números primos. ¿Cuáles de las opciones contienen solo primos?
- Pista 2: Divide 60 entre 2 repetidamente: 60 ÷ 2 = 30 ÷ 2 = 15. Ahora factoriza 15.
- Pista 3: 15 = 3 × 5. Entonces 60 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 5 = ?

P2
- Enunciado: ¿Cuál es el MCD de 24 y 36?
- Respuesta: 12
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Factoriza ambos números en factores primos.
- Pista 2: 24 = 2³ × 3 y 36 = 2² × 3². Busca los factores comunes con el menor exponente.
- Pista 3: Los factores comunes son 2² y 3. Multiplica: 4 × 3 = ?

P3
- Enunciado: ¿Cuál es el MCM de 4 y 6?
- Respuesta: 12
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Factoriza ambos números y toma todos los factores con el mayor exponente.
- Pista 2: 4 = 2² y 6 = 2 × 3. El mayor exponente de 2 es 2², y el de 3 es 3¹.
- Pista 3: MCM = 2² × 3 = 4 × 3 = ?

P4
- Enunciado: En la sucesión 3, 7, 11, 15... ¿cuál es el siguiente número?
- Respuesta: 19
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Identifica el patrón — ¿cuánto aumenta cada término?
- Pista 2: La diferencia entre términos consecutivos es siempre la misma. Calcula 7 − 3.
- Pista 3: La diferencia es 4. Suma 4 al último término: 15 + 4 = ?

P5
- Enunciado: ¿Cuántos múltiplos de 7 hay entre 1 y 50?
- Respuesta: 7
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Los múltiplos de 7 son: 7, 14, 21... Lista los que están entre 1 y 50.
- Pista 2: El mayor múltiplo de 7 que no supera 50 es 49. ¿Cuántos múltiplos hay desde 7 hasta 49?
- Pista 3: 7, 14, 21, 28, 35, 42, 49. Cuenta cuántos hay.

P6
- Enunciado: ¿Cuál es el MCD de 45 y 60?
- Respuesta: 15
- Tipo: open
- Pista 1: Factoriza ambos números en factores primos.
- Pista 2: 45 = 3² × 5 y 60 = 2² × 3 × 5. Busca los factores comunes.
- Pista 3: Los factores comunes son 3 y 5. Multiplica: 3 × 5 = ?

### REGLAS DE VALIDACIÓN ADICIONALES — PAA

#### Regla PAA-1 — Formato del examen
Los problemas PAA en Nuvo Math pueden ser:
- **open:** el estudiante escribe la respuesta numérica (validado por Algebrite o comparación exacta)
- **mcq:** selección múltiple con 4 opciones (A, B, C, D) — validado por coincidencia de letra

Los problemas de prueba (test) no tienen pistas.
Los problemas de práctica siempre tienen 3 pistas escaladas.

#### Regla PAA-2 — Idioma
Todo el contenido de la sección PAA está en español sin excepción.
Esto incluye enunciados, pistas, tarjetas de concepto, ejemplos trabajados y mensajes de retroalimentación.

#### Regla PAA-3 — Sin calculadora
Todos los problemas PAA deben ser resolubles sin calculadora.
Evitar números que requieran divisiones largas o decimales complejos.
Los números en problemas de porcentaje deben ser "amigables" (múltiplos de 5, 10, 25, etc.)

#### Regla PAA-4 — Contexto real
Al menos el 40% de los problemas de práctica deben tener contexto del mundo real
(precios, distancias, grupos de personas, situaciones cotidianas panameñas cuando sea posible).

#### Regla PAA-5 — Dificultad progresiva dentro del nivel
Dentro de cada nivel, los 6 problemas de práctica deben progresar de más fácil a más difícil.
P1-P2: aplicación directa del concepto
P3-P4: una operación adicional o contexto
P5-P6: problema de dos pasos o contexto más elaborado
